# IP policy: real names for Faithfuls, original names for Remixes, no ripped assets

The repo is public, and original game titles are live trademarks even when mechanics are freely clonable. Faithful versions reference the original name plainly ("Pong — a faithful recreation"), which is standard practice for educational clones and low-risk for arcade-era titles. Every Remix ships under an invented name of its own — it is our game, and shipping a modified game under someone else's trademark is the weakest legal position.

Assets follow the same posture: all art, sound, and fonts are self-made, properly licensed, or AI-generated from IP-safe prompts — never extracted from the originals. Faithful versions evoke the original's look and feel without copying its actual sprites or audio; distributing ripped assets in a public repo is straightforward infringement regardless of intent.

When the roadmap reaches titles from actively-defended franchises (e.g. Nintendo's Mario Kart), revisit this per title before shipping — consistent with the existing policy of keeping Pokémon work on a separate account.
